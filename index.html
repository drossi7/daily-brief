The Moment
First Light Across The Kitchen Floor

You know the stripe. Around 6:50 AM the sun clears the neighbour's roofline and a single diagonal of warm light drops across the kitchen floor. This morning is the first clear-sky Monday in a run of grey — use it. Get down low, camera on the floor plane, and wait for the stripe to land where you want it. Place your coffee mug inside the light, or let one of the dogs wander into it. Do not chase. Let the light come to you.

Compose with the stripe as a diagonal leading line — kitchen floor tiles or hardwood grain running with or against it. The highlight falls off fast, so meter for the brightest part of the stripe and let the shadow side collapse to deep charcoal. This is a negative space frame: one warm band of light, everything else quiet. Shoot, review, print, post. Clouds roll in by late afternoon so the window is sharp and short.

First clear morning in a week. I was pouring coffee and watched the sun clear the neighbour's roof and drop one stripe of warm light across the kitchen floor. Put the mug down. Got the camera. Got on the floor.

Forty-six minutes of golden light and I only needed one frame. TTArtisan 40mm wide open-ish, metered for the bright side, let the rest fall to charcoal. The mug inside the stripe. The dog in the doorway watching me act like a person who drops to the floor for a single band of light. Which I am.

Printed it on the Selphy while the light was still there. Held the print inside the same stripe of light it came from. That's the whole thing. The beauty was already here. I just waited long enough to see it. ⬜

Section 09
The Print
▾
Paper & Size
Canon KP-108IP
Glossy 4×6" dye-sub. One full cartridge = 108 prints. Fresh tray — don't feed curled stock.
Color Profile
Warm LUT + Cinelike D2
Export sRGB, darkest values RGB 10–15 (not 0) — dye-sub renders near-black richer than absolute black.
Mode & Finish
Borderless · Glossy Laminate
Standard quality, borderless. Laminate pass is the 4th — that's the satisfying slow-mo beat.
Filming beats 04 – 05: Set phone on a tabletop tripod beside the Selphy at a low 3/4 angle. Start recording before you hit print on the printer. Lock exposure on the paper slot. Record at 60fps so you can slow the laminate pass to 0.5×. For beat 05, carry the finished print to the kitchen floor stripe, hold it inside the same light it was made from, hold for two full seconds, hard cut. If the stripe of light has moved by then, crouch lower — the sun is tracking west fast.
