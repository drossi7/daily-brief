Section 02
Today's Shoot
▾
The Moment
sunlight through the fur at seven

It's one of those April mornings where the light arrives early and it's actually warm enough to let the dogs out without a jacket. Be in the backyard before 7:30 AM — your yard at that hour has its own version of golden hour that most people sleep through. The sun comes in low from the east, cuts across the deck boards, and when the dogs trot out into the grass, their fur catches it edge-lit and luminous. You're looking for the moment when one of them pauses — mid-sniff, mid-bound, mid-look-back — and the light wraps around their outline like it was placed deliberately.

Get low. Kneel in the wet April grass, hold the 40mm at waist height or lower, and let the dog fill the lower third of the frame while the yard recedes into soft-focus green. At f/2 and 1/1000s, the background dissolves completely. Don't chase the dog — let them come to you, or settle and wait. One decisive moment, one frame. You'll know when you have it before you check the LCD.

Settings
Primary Lens
TTArtisan 40mm f/2
Close focus of 35cm lets you get right into the dog's space. Wide-open rendering dissolves the backyard into painterly morning light.
Secondary Option
TTArtisan 75mm f/2
Compression option — flatten the background to pure bokeh, isolate fur texture from 3–4 metres. Strong for tighter portraits.
Exposure
f/2 · 1/1000s · ISO 400
Freezes any motion, opens shadow detail in fur. Daylight WB or Auto. Cinelike D2 profile for maximum dynamic range in the highlights.
BTS Filming
Phone on compact tripod, ground level
Wide enough to see you, the dog, and the yard. 4K/24fps. Position so the low east light is visible in the BTS frame too.

Section 09
The Print — Selphy Settings
▾
Paper & Size
Canon KP-108IP · 4×6"
Standard Selphy postcard stock. Full pack loaded. The dye-sub laminate layer locks in the warmth of fur tones and protects against fingerprints when held for Beat 05.
Color Profile
Auto Color / Natural
The warm morning tones are already in the shot — let the printer reproduce faithfully. Keep darkest values at RGB 10–15, not pure 0, for richest dye-sub shadow rendering.
Mode & Finish
Borderless · Standard
Borderless preferred — gives the fur and light maximum room to breathe to the edges. Standard finish over glossy for a more natural, matte-adjacent feel with this subject.
📹 Filming Beats 04–05
Set the Selphy on the kitchen counter with the backyard visible through the window behind it — sunny yard in the background contextualizes where the image came from. Set your phone in landscape on a small stand or stack of books to capture the paper emerging from the front slot. Film the paper feed at 240fps slow-mo; cut back to normal speed for the LCD display reveal. For Beat 05, take the finished print outside immediately — still slightly warm from the laminate — and hold it at dog-nose height in the same backyard. Dog in soft focus behind the print, morning light still catching the surface. One held frame, then hard cut to black.
