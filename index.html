SECTION 02
Today's Shoot
▼
THE MOMENT
First Gold Across the Floorboards

Post-sunrise, before the clouds thicken, there's a brief window where the low sun cuts through the east-facing window and lands across the floor in one clean horizontal stripe. Wait for it. Let a dog paw drift into the frame, or set your coffee mug down exactly at the edge of the light. That seam between shadow and gold is the photograph — don't crowd it.

Get low. Camera on the floor, eye-level with the light. 40mm wide open to melt the background, focus on the one object sitting in the brightest patch. The frame should feel half-empty. Negative space is the point — what you leave out tells the viewer where to look. Shoot fast; this light shifts every 90 seconds and is gone by 7:30.

SETTINGS
Primary
TTArtisan 40mm f/2 — wide open, shallow focus on the object inside the light stripe.
Secondary
TTArtisan 75mm f/2 — tighter compression if the paw or mug has room to breathe.
Exposure
ISO 400 · 1/400s · f/2.0 · Cinelike D2 — expose for the highlight, let the shadows fall.
BTS Filming
Phone on the full-height tripod, framed wide to catch you kneeling beside the light stripe. Slight backlight on you from the window — lean into the silhouette.

SECTION 09
The Print
▼
PAPER & SIZE
Canon KP-108IP
4×6" glossy dye-sub, from the standard cartridge. Horizontal orientation — let the light stripe run the long way across the print.
COLOR PROFILE
Cinelike D2 · warm
Pull shadows up 15, highlights down 10 so the gold stays readable on dye-sub. Keep the darkest values at RGB 10–15 — not pure black — for richest shadows on paper.
MODE & FINISH
Standard · Glossy
Full laminate pass. ~47 seconds through all four passes (cyan, magenta, yellow, laminate). Let it rest 30 seconds before handling.
Film beat 04 from directly above the Selphy output slot — paper emerging in slow-motion (120fps), cut to 0.4x playback. Beat 05: hold the finished print at arm's length inside the original light stripe on the floor, keep your thumb in frame on the lower-left corner so the print reads as a physical object in the world it came from. Hard cut to black on the "keep this frame" beat.
