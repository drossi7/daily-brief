Section 02
Today's Shoot
The Moment
First Flurries Meeting Wet Pavement
Calgary is trying to decide between winter and spring this morning, and you get to document the stalemate. Step out the front door with your coffee and wait. A 30% chance of flurries is exactly enough — the ones that make it down land on wet, nearly-black asphalt and disappear within a second. That disappearing is the photo.
Kneel low, frame the driveway as a dark negative-space canvas, and let a single flurry or small cluster fall into the right third. Let the neighbourhood blur out of focus behind. The grey-sky light is flat and kind — no shadows to fight, so you can push contrast into the edit later. Pull the dogs into frame if they wander over; their curious silhouette against the wet driveway is a bonus frame if you catch it.
Settings
Primary Lens
TTArtisan 40mm f/2 — close enough to hero a flurry without losing context of the driveway behind.
Secondary
TTArtisan 75mm f/2 for tight compression on a single flurry against wet pavement.
Exposure
f/2.8 · 1/500s · ISO 400 · WB 5600K
LUT
Cinelike D2 + custom cool-mute grade. Keep blacks near RGB 12.
BTS Filming
Phone on the compact tripod at porch height. Two angles: one wide profile of you kneeling, one locked-off of the Selphy on the kitchen counter.

Section 08
Caption & Hashtags

Late April in Calgary and I stepped outside expecting the sun. Got flurries instead — the kind that only exist for half a second before the wet driveway eats them. I almost went back inside. Then I knelt down.

One frame on the 40mm. f/2.8, 1/500s, ISO 400. I held my breath, waited for a flurry to fall into the right third of the driveway, and clicked. The Selphy took 47 seconds to walk the paper through its four passes. I stood in the kitchen holding a coffee that had already gone cold, watching it come out line by line.

The last flurries of a Calgary winter are the ones nobody photographs. Nobody prints. That's exactly why this one is worth keeping. ⬜

Section 09
The Print
Paper & Size
Canon KP-108IP · 4×6"
Standard postcard size for everyday frames. Dye-sub layer keeps the print water-safe once cooled — fine for holding in light flurries during beat 05.
Color Profile
Cinelike D2 · Custom LUT
Keep darkest values around RGB 10–15 rather than pure 0. Dye-sub renders near-black richer than absolute black. Lift shadows +8, pull highlights -6.
Mode & Finish
Borderless · Glossy
Single copy. Borderless to let the wet-pavement frame read as all texture. 47-second print time — plan your BTS tilt and slow-mo around it.
Filming note for beats 04–05: Lock the camera on the compact tripod at the kitchen counter, angled 45° down onto the Selphy slot. Record in 4K 60fps so you can slow the paper-emerge to 24fps in post. For beat 05, step back outside with the finished print and hold it at arm's length against the same patch of driveway — match the framing from beat 01 so the cut lands clean.
