Fog Breaks Over Princes Island Bow

Scene & Location
The Concept

Head to the east footbridge at Princes Island Park before 7 AM while the fog advisory is still active. Position at the mid-point of the footbridge looking west toward the RiverWalk pavilion — water-level fog will sit low between the banks, and the leafless early-spring cottonwoods will read as dark silhouettes cutting through silver-grey murk. The Bow is just beginning its spring melt: the current is high and dark, the banks still winter-bare. This is a scene of pure tone and texture with zero colour distraction.

Work the 21mm wide at f/4 for deep fog layers and maximum depth, from the railing cables to the far bank. The tension is in the horizontal fog layer — keep your horizon low in the frame, two-thirds sky, so the fog band reads as a thick grey stripe separating pale sky from near-black water below. One frame: the one where a bridge cable runs diagonally corner to corner, and the cottonwood silhouettes are just barely distinct through the haze. If fog is still dense at 7:54 AM golden hour, stay — diffused light through fog is rarely this clean in April.

Settings
Camera Recipe
Primary Lens
TTArtisan 21mm f/1.5 — wide captures the full fog layer bank to bank; the diagonal of the bridge cable creates natural leading lines through the mist. Fog flattens contrast so you need the geometry.
Secondary Option
TTArtisan 75mm f/2 — from the riverbank path, compress a single cottonwood trunk emerging from fog; shallow depth turns the fog into a soft silver wash behind the bark texture.
Exposure Settings
f/4 · ISO 800 · 1/250s (morning fog) — expose for the sky to keep fog tones cool and grey; let foreground go dark for contrast. Evening: f/2 · ISO 400 · 1/1000s for warm golden hour.
BTS Filming Note
Full-height tripod 1.5m behind and slightly right of shooting position — frame Dario composing at the railing, hands on lens, then the EVF moment before shutter. Use 120fps slow-mo for the shutter-fire beat.

The Print — Filming Guide
▲
Paper & Size
Canon KP-108IP
4×6" · Standard dye-sublimation · 108-sheet pack
Color Profile
Natural
Avoid Vivid — fog scene benefits from neutral, desaturated tones. Natural preserves the cool silver-grey palette.
Mode & Finish
Standard · Glossy
Glossy surface catches light when held over the river in Beat 05 — the reflection works better with gloss than matte.
Filming Beats 04–05: Step-by-Step
Beat 04 — Place the Selphy on a flat, clean surface (kitchen counter or table). Position your filming camera at roughly 45° to the printer's feed slot so both the Selphy display screen and the emerging paper are in frame simultaneously. Record at 120fps for slow-mo — the full print cycle is about 90 seconds, but 20 seconds of slow-mo paper emerging is all you need. Duck your music to 20% at the 0:19 mark; bring up light ambient room tone underneath for texture and presence.

Beat 05 — Carry the still-warm 4×6 print back to the Princes Island footbridge railing (or any location with dark, moving water visible below). Hold the print horizontal at arm's length over the railing, glossy side facing down toward the water. Lower it slowly and steadily — at roughly 1 metre above the water the reflection will begin to appear in the current below. The moment the reflection sharpens into a recognizable duplicate of the fog scene on the print surface — hard cut to black. Do not fade. Cut immediately.
