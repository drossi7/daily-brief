Scene & Location
East Village RiverWalk, Calgary AB

The East Village RiverWalk stretches between the Bow River and 9 Avenue SE — a quiet concrete promenade that transforms completely under fresh snow. Position yourself at the bend near 5 Street SE where the modern balustrades frame the dark, still-flowing Bow beneath a white-dusted sky. The contrasts are stark today: black river water against white banks, leafless trees catching new snow, the glass towers of East Village dissolving softly in the grey. Get low — a ground-level composition looking west toward the Centre Street Bridge puts fresh snowfall on the railing in the foreground, with the city ghosting behind.

There's no golden hour to chase today, and that's the point. The overcast turns the sky into an enormous diffusion panel — zero harsh shadows, a delicate luminosity across every surface. The TTArtisan 40mm at f/5.6 handles this beautifully. Set your exposure +1 to +1.3 stops above meter reading so the snow renders white, not grey. Move slowly. A single bare branch bent under snow weight against the river's far bank is a better frame than a wide landscape today — the restraint is the story.

Filming Beats 04 & 05 — Director's Notes
For beat 04, set your phone to 4K 120fps before the Selphy starts printing — you want the paper emergence in full slow motion. Duck all audio on this clip; the silence before the music swell lands harder than any SFX. Keep the Selphy label visible in the lower-right corner of frame so the gear is readable. For beat 05, take the finished print back outside to the RiverWalk immediately while conditions hold. Find a dark snowmelt puddle on the concrete path — look for the ones near the railing edges where the snow is thicker. Hold the print face-down horizontally at about 30cm above the water surface. The glossy finish will create a clean mirror reflection. Steady your hand and hold the shot for a full 3 seconds before the hard cut to black.
