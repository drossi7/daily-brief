Scene & Location
The Concept

Head to the Bow River pathway at St. Patrick's Island, entering from the East Village side via the footbridge at the end of Murdoch Avenue SE. Target the shallow west-shore inlet on the island itself — in mid-April, spring meltwater from the Rockies raises the Bow just enough to flood the gravel bar that sits dry in summer, creating a wide, mirror-flat pool just below knee height. Arrive at 6:17 AM during blue hour while the East Village towers are still lit and the sky is a deep gradient of indigo fading toward orange at the horizon. This double-light moment — city warm from above, sky cold from behind — is unique to spring mornings here.

Set the compact tripod with the TTArtisan 40mm just 25–30 cm above the waterline and angle the lens slightly downward so the reflection dominates the bottom two-thirds of the frame. You are composing two skies — the real one above and the inverted one below, separated only by the razor line of the water surface. When the first hard edge of sun crests the roofline of the towers at exactly 6:47 AM, fire immediately — that 10-second window where the disc of the sun reflects as a perfect ellipse in still water is today's one beautiful moment. The 40mm's field of view is wide enough to anchor you in the location while tight enough to eliminate the distracting riverbank.

Settings
Camera Recipe
Primary Lens
TTArtisan 40mm f/2 — balances intimacy and context; wide enough to show sky, water and faint skyline, tight enough to lock on the reflection quality
Secondary Option
TTArtisan 75mm f/2 — isolate the compressed city reflection across the far bank; dramatic bokeh separates towers from water surface
Exposure Settings
Blue hour: f/2.8 · 1/60s · ISO 1600 · AWB +150K warm push → Sunrise: f/5.6 · 1/500s · ISO 200 · keep AWB warm. Expose for the reflection, let the sky blow slightly.
BTS Filming Note
Full-height tripod 1.5 m behind, aimed slightly down to frame you + compact tripod + glowing water surface simultaneously. Shoot BTS at 60fps. Stay silent — only ambient river sound during beats 02–03.

Print Process

Camera body set on the flat rock beside the water. Cut to the Canon Selphy CP1500 display on the riverbank, the image queued. Duck the audio 3 dB. The Print — Filming Guide
▲
Paper & Size
KP-108IP · 4×6"
Canon Selphy standard pack · glossy surface · 108 sheets per pack
Color Profile
Vivid
Boosts the indigo-to-orange sky gradient and deepens the water reflection blues. Critical for today's shoot — Natural mode will flatten the gradient.
Mode & Finish
Bordered · Glossy
Classic photo-print look with white border — reinforces the physical artifact in beat 05 when held over the water reflection
Beat 04–05 Filming Instructions
Set the Selphy CP1500 on a flat rock or closed camera bag on the riverbank so it's stable. For beat 04, lock BTS camera on the paper output slot at a 45° angle — you want the emerging paper to fill at least 40% of the BTS frame. Shoot at 60fps and slow to 30% in edit so viewers can watch the dye-sublimation process in detail, colour band by colour band. For beat 05, position yourself standing with your back to the water, Bow River visible below and behind you in the frame. Hold the finished print horizontally at waist height, printed face downward toward the river surface. The alignment — print image matching the actual reflection beneath — is the final image of the Reel. Start recording before you raise the print and hold position until the alignment is obvious, then cut to black on the locked frame. Do not move the print once aligned; let the cut do the work.
