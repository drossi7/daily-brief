Today's Shoot
Coffee Steam Against the Window Snow
▲
The Moment
Your kitchen. No travel required.

Find the window with the best view of the snow — ideally one where flakes are still falling, or fresh accumulation sits on the outer sill. Set your mug down on the table or windowsill so the window fills the background cleanly. The TTArtisan 40mm at f/2 will render the cup and rising steam sharp while the snow and sky behind the glass go beautifully soft. Look for the steam catching the flat grey morning light — cold windows and warm coffee create a natural updraft that's visible from the right angle.

This frame isn't about the coffee. It's about the feeling of being warm inside while Calgary does this outside. Get low — shooting from counter height keeps the window large and the composition honest. If the dogs wander into the background, let them blur in there. Expose for the window (the brightest element) and let the interior read slightly dark — you want that warm-vs-cold tension, not a blown white background. The mood you're after is: quiet, early, the city hasn't started yet.

Settings
Camera Recipe
Primary Lens
TTArtisan 40mm f/2 — minimum 35cm focus lets you get right up to the rim, steam in sharp focus, snow blurred behind
Secondary Option
TTArtisan 75mm f/2 — from the far end of the kitchen for full compression, window completely abstract
Exposure Settings
f/2 · ISO 800 · 1/200s — expose for the window, let interior go warm and moody. Shoot RAW.
BTS Filming
Compact tripod on the counter behind you at 4K — capture composing + focus ring turning + shutter + LCD reveal
